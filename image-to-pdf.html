FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Which image formats can I convert to PDF?
This tool supports JPG/JPEG, PNG, WebP, GIF, and BMP images. Each image is placed on its own page in the resulting PDF. For WebP and GIF, the image is first converted to PNG via an HTML canvas element before embedding in the PDF.
Can I convert multiple images into one PDF?
Yes! Add multiple images and they will each be placed on a separate page in the output PDF, in the order shown in the list. You can remove individual images before converting using the ✕ button on each item.
What page sizes are supported?
You can choose A4 (595×842 points / 210×297 mm), US Letter (612×792 points / 8.5×11 in), or Original Size (uses the image's natural pixel dimensions as the page size). Portrait and landscape orientations are available for A4 and Letter.
Is my image uploaded to any server?
No. All conversion happens in your browser using the pdf-lib JavaScript library. Your images are never uploaded to our servers — they stay completely private on your device throughout the entire process.
Will the image quality be preserved?
JPG and PNG images are embedded directly into the PDF at their original quality without re-compression. WebP and GIF are first rasterized to PNG via canvas, which preserves full quality. The resulting PDF contains the image at its original resolution.
